# Metrics Checklist

Use this checklist to identify useful metrics for a candidate automation opportunity.

The aim is not to collect every possible metric. The aim is to identify enough evidence to understand the pain, test assumptions and decide what should happen next.

> **Map the work. Measure the pain. Automate with purpose.**

---

## When to use this checklist

Use this checklist when:

* a process feels painful but the evidence is unclear
* the team needs to understand whether the pain is effort, flow, quality, risk or user impact
* an automation idea needs to be tested with better evidence
* multiple automation opportunities need to be compared
* baseline data is needed before improvement or automation
* a team needs to identify what evidence is missing

---

## How to use this checklist

1. Start with the process map or scenario description.
2. Identify the type of pain being experienced.
3. Review the metric groups: **effort**, **flow**, and **quality and risk**.
4. Capture what is known.
5. Mark estimates clearly.
6. Identify missing evidence.
7. Use the outputs to support the automation opportunity assessment.

You do not need every metric. Choose the metrics that best explain the pain and value.

## Final reminder

Do not collect metrics for the sake of it.

Use metrics to answer three practical questions:

1. Where is the pain?
2. How significant is it?
3. What response is most likely to help?

> **You do not need every metric — just the ones that help explain the pain and value.**
