# Automation Opportunity Assessment

Use this template to assess whether a candidate process or workflow is a good automation opportunity.

The assessment uses three lenses:

1. **Value** — is this worth solving?
2. **Feasibility** — can this be automated?
3. **Readiness** — is the process ready?

The aim is not to create a full business case or technical design. The aim is to make a clear, evidence-based recommendation about what should happen next.

> **Map the work. Measure the pain. Automate with purpose.**

---

## When to use this template

Use this template when:

* a process has been identified as a possible automation candidate
* the team has completed an initial process map or scenario discussion
* there is some evidence about pain, effort, delay, risk or rework
* the team needs to decide whether to automate, improve, investigate or defer
* multiple opportunities need to be compared
* a lightweight decision record is needed before deeper discovery

---

## How to use this template

1. Summarise the process or opportunity.
2. Capture the known pain points and evidence.
3. Assess the opportunity against value, feasibility and readiness.
4. Identify risks, assumptions and missing information.
5. Select an initial opportunity category.
6. Agree the next best action.

This assessment should be updated as new evidence becomes available.

## Final reminder

A good automation candidate is not just technically possible.

It should be:

* valuable enough to prioritise
* feasible enough to automate
* ready enough to change

If an opportunity is not ready for automation, it may still be worth improving.

> **Map the work. Measure the pain. Automate with purpose.**
