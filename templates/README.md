# Templates

This folder contains reusable templates for the **Metrics Based Process Mapping Workshop**.

The templates are designed to help teams apply the workshop method consistently before, during and after the session.

> **Map the work. Measure the pain. Automate with purpose.**

---

## Purpose of the templates

The templates help teams move from broad automation ideas to clearer, evidence-based opportunities.

They are intended to support four activities:

1. **Map the work** — understand how the process happens today.
2. **Measure the pain** — capture lightweight metrics about effort, flow, quality, risk and user impact.
3. **Decide the response** — determine whether automation, improvement, standardisation or further investigation is the right next step.
4. **Prioritise the opportunity** — identify which candidates are valuable, feasible and ready to take forward.

These templates are deliberately lightweight. They are not intended to replace detailed process analysis, solution design or delivery planning.

## Recommended workflow

Use the templates in this order.

```text
1. process-mapping-canvas.md
        ↓
2. metrics-checklist.md
        ↓
3. scenario-clinic-capture.md
        ↓
4. automation-opportunity-assessment.md
        ↓
5. opportunity-backlog.md
```

The workflow does not need to be rigid. Use the templates that fit the maturity of the team and the level of detail required.

## 1. Process mapping canvas

File:

```text
process-mapping-canvas.md
```

Use this template to capture the basics of a process:

* process name
* trigger
* outcome
* main steps
* roles involved
* systems used
* decision points
* handoffs
* exceptions
* known pain points

This template is useful when the team needs to create a shared view of how work happens today.

### Use when

* the process is not clearly understood
* different people describe the process differently
* there are multiple teams or handoffs involved
* the team is considering automation but has not mapped the work
* the group needs to separate the normal path from exceptions

### Output

A simple current-state process view that can be tested with people who do the work.

---

## 2. Metrics checklist

File:

```text
metrics-checklist.md
```

Use this template to identify which metrics may help explain the pain and value of the opportunity.

The checklist groups metrics into:

* effort
* flow
* quality and risk

### Use when

* the team has identified a painful process but lacks evidence
* there is a need to separate perception from measurable impact
* the team needs to compare multiple opportunities
* baseline data is needed before automation or improvement

### Output

A short list of known metrics, estimates and missing evidence.

---

## 3. Scenario clinic capture

File:

```text
scenario-clinic-capture.md
```

Use this template during the workshop to capture discussion about a real candidate process.

It helps the facilitator and group answer:

* What is the process?
* Where does it start and end?
* Where is the pain?
* What evidence do we have?
* What are we assuming?
* Is the likely response automation, improvement or both?
* What is the next best action?

### Use when

* running the scenario clinic section of the workshop
* testing a candidate automation idea live with a group
* deciding whether a process should be automated, improved, investigated or deferred

### Output

A structured record of the scenario discussion and agreed next action.

---

## 4. Automation opportunity assessment

File:

```text
automation-opportunity-assessment.md
```

Use this template to assess whether an opportunity is worth taking forward.

The assessment uses three lenses:

| Lens            | Key question           |
| --------------- | ---------------------- |
| **Value**       | Is this worth solving? |
| **Feasibility** | Can we automate it?    |
| **Readiness**   | Is the process ready?  |

### Use when

* the team has a candidate opportunity and needs to decide what happens next
* there are multiple opportunities to compare
* the process may be painful but not ready for automation
* a lightweight assessment is needed before deeper discovery

### Output

A clear recommendation:

* automate now
* improve first
* investigate further
* do not automate yet

---

## 5. Opportunity backlog

File:

```text
opportunity-backlog.md
```

Use this template after the workshop to track candidate automation opportunities.

It captures:

* process name
* pain point
* known evidence
* missing evidence
* likely response
* opportunity category
* next best action
* owner
* status

### Use when

* the team has several candidate opportunities
* workshop outputs need to be turned into follow-up actions
* opportunities need to be prioritised and tracked
* the team wants to build a small evidence-based automation backlog

### Output

A lightweight automation opportunity backlog that can be reviewed and refined over time.

## Opportunity categories

The templates use four simple categories.

| Category                | Meaning                                      | Typical next action                                |
| ----------------------- | -------------------------------------------- | -------------------------------------------------- |
| **Automate now**        | High value, feasible and ready               | Move into automation discovery or design           |
| **Improve first**       | Worth solving, but process needs tidying     | Clarify, simplify, standardise or assign ownership |
| **Investigate further** | Potential value, but more evidence is needed | Gather data or assess technical feasibility        |
| **Do not automate yet** | Low value, unstable or unclear               | Defer or address underlying process issues         |

Remember:

> Not ready for automation does not mean not worth improving.

## Metrics used in the templates

The templates use three practical metric groups.

### Effort

| Metric            | What it helps show            |
| ----------------- | ----------------------------- |
| **Volume**        | How often the process happens |
| **Touch time**    | Actual human effort involved  |
| **Manual effort** | Repetitive or low-value work  |

### Flow

| Metric           | What it helps show                                   |
| ---------------- | ---------------------------------------------------- |
| **Cycle time**   | Total elapsed time from start to finish              |
| **Wait time**    | Time spent idle between steps                        |
| **Handoffs**     | Movement between people or teams                     |
| **Systems used** | Tools, forms or platforms touched across the process |

### Quality and risk

| Metric              | What it helps show                              |
| ------------------- | ----------------------------------------------- |
| **Rework / errors** | How often work needs correction                 |
| **Exceptions**      | Deviations from the standard path               |
| **Risk impact**     | What happens if something goes wrong            |
| **User impact**     | Who experiences the pain, delay or poor outcome |

You do not need every metric for every process. Choose the metrics that best explain the pain and value.

## Placeholder conventions

Square brackets indicate content that should be customised.

| Placeholder       | What it represents                                 |
| ----------------- | -------------------------------------------------- |
| `[PROCESS NAME]`  | Candidate process being mapped                     |
| `[PROCESS OWNER]` | Person or team accountable for the process         |
| `[TEAM]`          | Team involved in the process                       |
| `[SYSTEM]`        | Tool, system, form or platform used in the process |
| `[METRIC]`        | Evidence or measure to be validated                |
| `[OWNER]`         | Owner of an action or follow-up                    |
| `[DATE]`          | Date captured or reviewed                          |
| `[NEXT ACTION]`   | Agreed next best action                            |
| `[STATUS]`        | Current status of the opportunity or action        |

## How much detail is enough?

These templates are designed for discovery, not detailed design.

A good template response should be:

* clear enough to understand the process
* specific enough to identify the pain
* evidence-based where possible
* honest about assumptions
* clear about missing data
* focused on the next best action

Avoid trying to fully solve or design the automation inside these templates.

---

## Suggested use after the workshop

After the workshop, use the completed templates to:

1. Validate the process with people who do the work.
2. Gather missing metrics.
3. Confirm the opportunity category.
4. Decide whether the process should move into automation discovery, process improvement or further investigation.
5. Add the opportunity to the backlog if it is worth tracking.
