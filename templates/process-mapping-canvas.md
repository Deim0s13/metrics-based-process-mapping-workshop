# Process Mapping Canvas

Use this canvas to capture a process at a practical discovery level.

The aim is not to create a perfect process map. The aim is to create a shared view of how the work happens today so the team can identify pain points, useful metrics and possible automation opportunities.

> **Map the work. Measure the pain. Automate with purpose.**

---

## When to use this canvas

Use this canvas when:

* a process may be a candidate for automation
* the process is not clearly understood end to end
* different people describe the process differently
* there are multiple teams, systems or handoffs involved
* the team needs to identify where pain, delay, rework or risk exists
* the team needs to decide whether to automate, improve, investigate or defer

---

## How to use this canvas

1. Start with the **normal path** through the process.
2. Capture the **trigger**, **outcome**, **main steps**, **roles** and **systems**.
3. Add **decision points**, **handoffs** and **exceptions**.
4. Identify known **pain points**.
5. Capture any known **metrics or evidence**.
6. Note any assumptions or missing information.
7. Use the output to support the scenario clinic or automation opportunity assessment.

A useful map is better than a perfect map.
