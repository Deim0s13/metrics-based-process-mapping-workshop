# Scenario Clinic Capture

Use this template during the scenario clinic section of the Metrics Based Process Mapping workshop.

The purpose of the scenario clinic is to test a real process or automation idea using the workshop method.

The goal is not to fully solve the process in the room. The goal is to understand the opportunity well enough to decide the next best action.

> **Map the work. Measure the pain. Automate with purpose.**

---

## When to use this template

Use this template when:

* running the scenario clinic section of the workshop
* testing a candidate automation idea with a group
* deciding whether a process should be automated, improved, investigated or deferred
* capturing assumptions, missing evidence and follow-up actions
* turning a workshop discussion into a useful record

---

## How to use this template

1. Select one scenario or candidate process.
2. Define where the process starts and ends.
3. Identify the pain points.
4. Capture known evidence and assumptions.
5. Discuss whether the likely response is automation, improvement or both.
6. Categorise the opportunity.
7. Agree the next best action.

Keep the discussion at discovery level. Do not try to design the full automation solution in the session.

Use a quick assessment to test the opportunity.

| Lens            | Key question           | Assessment                    | Notes     |
| --------------- | ---------------------- | ----------------------------- | --------- |

| **Readiness**   | Is the process ready?  | High / Medium / Low / Unknown | `[NOTES]` |

## Quick prompts

### Value

* How significant is the pain?
* How often does this happen?
* Who benefits if this improves?
* What risk, delay, rework or effort could be reduced?

### Feasibility

* Are the rules clear?
* Are inputs and outputs stable?
* Is the data accessible?
* Are systems accessible through workflow, integration, export or automation tooling?
* Are exceptions manageable?

### Readiness

* Is there a process owner?
* Is the process stable?
* Are stakeholders aligned?
* Are approval or decision rules clear?
* Are there dependencies to resolve first?

## Final reminder

The scenario clinic is not about solving everything in the room.

It is about answering:

1. What is the process?
2. Where is the pain?
3. What evidence do we have?
4. Is the likely response automation, improvement or both?
5. What is the next best action?
