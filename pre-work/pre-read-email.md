Subject

Pre-work: Metrics Based Process Mapping workshop

⸻

Email

Hi team,

Ahead of our Metrics Based Process Mapping workshop on [DATE], please come prepared with one process, activity or workflow that may be a candidate for automation.

It does not need to be fully documented or perfectly understood. A good example might be something that is:

* manual
* repetitive
* slow
* high-volume
* risky
* inconsistent
* difficult to track
* approval-heavy
* spreadsheet-heavy
* dependent on email handoffs
* creating rework, errors or frustration

The aim of the session is not to jump straight to automation. We will use your examples to understand how the work happens today, where the pain is, what evidence we have, and whether automation is the right response.

Before the session, please think about the following questions for your example:

1. What starts the process?
2. What outcome is needed?
3. Who is involved?
4. What systems, tools, forms or spreadsheets are used?
5. Where does the process slow down?
6. Where is manual effort involved?
7. What tends to go wrong?
8. Where does rework or duplication happen?
9. What data or metrics do you already have?
10. What evidence would help us decide whether this is worth automating?

You do not need to prepare a presentation. A few notes are enough.

During the workshop we will use a lightweight approach called Metrics Based Process Mapping to explore a few examples. The core idea is simple:

Map the work. Measure the pain. Automate with purpose.

By the end of the session, the goal is to have a clearer way to assess automation opportunities and identify which candidates are worth taking forward, improving first, investigating further or deferring.

Thanks,

[FACILITATOR]
